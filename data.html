Open science

Community access and science-ready data

SPARK is designed as an open solar physics mission, with rapid release of processed data products and software for quantitative analysis.

Data policy

No proprietary period for science data

In line with common practice in solar physics, SPARK Level 0, quicklook, Level 1 and Level 2 data products will be made freely available to the science community after processing and verification by the instrument teams.

Products will use FITS formats with headers designed for distribution through virtual observatories and archives.

QuicklookBrowse flare intervals and instrument state
Level 1Calibrated data suitable for quantitative analysis
Level 2Science-ready products including reconstructed images
SoftwareCalibration, correction and reconstruction tools

Archives

Designed for discovery and coordinated science

SPARK data will be delivered to ESA archives and partner data centres. The mission concept includes integration with advanced solar data interfaces to support multi-mission comparison and coordinated analysis.
