Glossary

Key terms used on the SPARK website

Short definitions for acronyms and physical concepts used across the SPARK science and mission pages.

EUV

Extreme ultraviolet radiation, used by HiFI to image hot and transition-region solar plasma.

HXR

Hard X-rays, produced mainly by energetic electrons through bremsstrahlung in solar flares.

SXR

Soft X-rays, often associated with hot thermal flare plasma.

γ-ray

Gamma-ray emission, used by SPARK to diagnose energetic ions and high-energy particle populations.

Pion decay

A high-energy γ-ray signature produced when relativistic ions interact with dense solar material.

Bremsstrahlung

Radiation emitted when charged particles, especially electrons, are decelerated by interactions with matter.

Magnetic reconnection

A process in which magnetic field topology changes and magnetic energy is converted into heat, flows and energetic particles.

Plasmoid

A magnetic island or blob-like structure associated with fragmented reconnection in current sheets.

QPP

Quasi-periodic pulsations: repeated intensity variations observed during solar flares.

L1

The Sun–Earth L1 Lagrange point, a stable observing location between Earth and the Sun.

Transition region

The solar atmospheric layer between the chromosphere and corona.

Flare ribbon

Bright elongated emission in the lower solar atmosphere tracing energy deposition during flares.

Flare kernel

Compact, rapidly evolving bright structure within a flare ribbon.

Chromospheric evaporation

Upward flow of heated plasma from the lower atmosphere into flare loops.

Coronal rain

Cool, dense plasma condensations that fall along magnetic loops during post-flare cooling.
