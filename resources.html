Public explainer

SPARK in one page

SPARK — Solar Particle Acceleration, Radiation and Kinetics is an ESA M-class mission concept designed to understand how solar flares accelerate particles and heat plasma.

Solar flares are the nearest explosive particle accelerators that can be resolved in space and time. They release stored magnetic energy, accelerate electrons and ions, produce intense X-ray and γ-ray emission, and reshape the solar atmosphere.

LASSAN measures the particles through X-ray and γ-ray diagnostics, including bremsstrahlung from accelerated electrons, nuclear γ-ray lines from accelerated ions, and pion-decay emission from relativistic ions.

HiFI observes the plasma response through high-cadence, high-resolution EUV imaging of flare ribbons, kernels, hot loops, waves, flows and reconnection structures.

Together, LASSAN and HiFI connect the full chain from magnetic energy release to particle acceleration, energy transport and atmospheric response. SPARK therefore uses the Sun as a natural laboratory for particle acceleration in magnetised plasma.
