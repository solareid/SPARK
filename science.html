Science case

Understanding explosive energy conversion in magnetised plasma

SPARK addresses the physical chain from magnetic reconnection to accelerated particles, plasma heating, radiation and atmospheric response.

Astrophysical context

The Sun is the only high-energy particle accelerator we can resolve directly.

Non-thermal particle acceleration is central to the high-energy Universe, from supernova remnant shocks to relativistic jets. Solar flares provide the uniquely accessible case where the acceleration site, energetic particles and plasma response can be observed together.

Central question

How do magnetised plasmas explosively convert stored magnetic energy into accelerated particles and heated plasma?

Science themes

From flare onset to post-flare evolution

Particle acceleration

SPARK will test DC-field, stochastic, shock, collapsing-trap and plasmoid/island acceleration models by combining spectral fingerprints with flare geometry.

Energy transport

SPARK will distinguish particle beams, conduction and wave-driven transport by measuring timing and temperature evolution across flare ribbons, kernels and loops.

Reconnection, waves and instabilities

SPARK will determine whether rapid flare variability arises from intermittent reconnection, wave modulation or coupled wave-particle processes.

Science objectives

Eight objectives trace science to measurement

SO-1Determine the contribution of different acceleration mechanisms to electron and ion acceleration.
SO-2Determine the temporal evolution of electron and ion acceleration efficiencies and energy content.
SO-3Determine the nature of flare precursor events and heating, including HOPEs and nanojets.
SO-4Determine the spatial and temporal variation of energy dissipation during the impulsive phase.
SO-5Determine how superheated thermal plasmas evolve and dissipate energy during the gradual phase.
SO-6Determine what drives rapid variability and quasi-periodic pulsations in solar flares.
SO-7Determine the role of fine-scale structures and plasma instabilities in particle acceleration.
SO-8Characterise the coronal wave response and determine its causal coupling to particle acceleration.

What SPARK measures

Particles and plasma, simultaneously

LASSAN measures energetic electrons and ions through X-ray and γ-ray signatures. HiFI observes the flare atmosphere in multiple EUV passbands, revealing where energy is deposited and how the plasma responds.

ElectronsSoft and hard X-ray bremsstrahlung
IonsNuclear de-excitation lines, neutron capture and pion-decay signatures
PlasmaTransition-region, coronal and hot flare emission
DynamicsRibbons, kernels, plasmoids, waves, loop retraction and rain
