Payload

LASSAN and HiFI: particles and plasma together

SPARK’s payload is built around a single measurement philosophy: identify the accelerated particles and observe the atmospheric response at the same time.

γ

LASSAN

Large Area Spectrometer for Solar Accelerated Nuclei

LASSAN provides γ-ray and X-ray spectroscopy from 4 keV to more than 100 MeV, allowing simultaneous measurement of hot plasma, non-thermal electrons, MeV ions and relativistic ions.

EUV

HiFI

High Resolution Flare Imager

HiFI delivers high-cadence, high-resolution EUV imaging of flare ribbons, kernels, loops, waves and plasma flows while avoiding the saturation that limits current flare observations.

Payload synergy

The combined measurement is the breakthrough

LASSAN identifies particle populations and their evolving energy content. HiFI shows the evolving reconnection geometry and plasma response. Together, they provide the first direct observational framework for mechanism attribution in solar flare particle acceleration.

Spectra

Separate thermal, non-thermal, nuclear-line and pion-decay components across the keV–MeV–GeV domain.

Timing

Compare electron and ion timing with EUV flare-kernel, ribbon and loop dynamics.

Location

Use Co-STIX and HiFI to relate particle precipitation to ribbons, looptops, current sheets and evolving flare structures.
